The Book

From Backpacker to Business Builder

In the 1980s, long before online booking and budget airlines, Jerry Bridge set out to build a travel company from a scrappy office in Camden Town.


What followed was a twenty-year journey through risk, relentless pressure, and the shifting world of long-haul travel — where survival depended on instinct, relationships, and nerve.


Travelman is the story of what it takes to build something from nothing — and what it costs along the way. It's a story about a father's legacy, a passion for travel that became an obsession, and the grit behind every departure board and airline deal.

The Journey

Twenty Years of Risk, Travel & Ambition

The Beginning

A Father's Legacy

Jerry's father Walter was an early pioneer of UK travel. His company, Swiss Travel Service, established in 1949, was among the first to offer travel to everyday people after the Second World War. He encouraged Jerry to buy his first backpack and go hitch-hiking at sixteen.

The Adventure

22 Months Overland to Australia

After university, Jerry embarked on an incredible 22-month overland trip to Australia — without mobile phone, guidebook, or credit card. A 24,000 km road trip in a beat-up Holden station wagon. The kind of adventure that no longer exists.

Camden Town

Building Bridge the World

From a scrappy office in Camden Town, Jerry built Bridge the World into a travel powerhouse — 150 staff, turning over £50 million, selling flights and holidays to Australia, New Zealand, and Round the World itineraries. All built on instinct, relationships, and nerve.

Recognition

Awards & Industry Impact

Entrepreneur of the Year. OzCARS Winner for Outstanding Contribution to the Australian Tourism Industry. Multiple travel awards. The industry recognised what Jerry had built — and the travel trade would never be quite the same.

About the Author

Jerry Bridge

Jerry Bridge turned a passion for travel into a career spanning three decades. From backpacking across Europe at sixteen to building one of the UK's leading long-haul travel agencies, his story is one of relentless ambition, risk-taking, and the kind of entrepreneurial grit you can't learn from a textbook.

Today, Jerry lives in Wellington, New Zealand, where he continues to work in the travel industry through New Zealand Vacations and What to do in Wellington. Travelman is his first book — a memoir that's part business story, part travel adventure, and part love letter to a world that no longer exists.

His mission is simple: to inspire people to fulfil their dreams. To go for it. Get their hands dirty. To prove that with faith, determination and hard work, anything is possible.
